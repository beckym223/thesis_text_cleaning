This list may be suggestive of the actions that may have to be taken when, after years  of ease and growth, the firm finds its profits declining or disappearing.
'Perhaps there ought to be a place on the list for some gratifications that are more
stable, less subject to quantitative variation, such as the pleasure of being known for  honesty and fairness, on the one hand, and for sharpness and shrewdness, on the other,  or at least the pleasure of being convinced of having and exercising these qualities. And  last, though not least, there is the general feeling of gratification from "running" a large,  well-known profitable, widely respected firm with growing assets and employment.
